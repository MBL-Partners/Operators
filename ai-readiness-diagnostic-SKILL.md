# AI Readiness Diagnostic

A structured 10-minute diagnostic that scores your team's AI readiness across the six dimensions that most often decide whether an AI initiative sticks or stalls. It produces a readiness snapshot: what's working, where the gaps are, and 1-3 specific actions to take before committing budget.

---

## How to Run It

Trigger it by saying any of the following:

- *"Run the readiness diagnostic"*
- *"How ready is my team for AI?"*
- *"AI maturity assessment"*
- *"Should we start an AI initiative?"*

Ask the six scored questions one at a time, waiting for each response before moving on. Then ask the two context questions. Keep the tone conversational, not clinical. After all answers are collected, score questions 1-6 and produce the readiness snapshot.

### The Six Questions

**Question 1: Security**
"When your team uses AI tools, how is your data protected?"

- A. No guidance, people paste whatever they want into public AI tools (1 pt)
- B. Informal warnings exist, but there's no classification of what's safe to share (2 pts)
- C. We have a data classification policy, but it isn't consistently applied to AI use (3 pts)
- D. Clear, enforced rules govern what data goes into which tools, with an incident path if something slips (4 pts)

**Question 2: Integration**
"How is AI connected across your team's workflows and platforms?"

- A. It isn't, any use is personal and one-off (1 pt)
- B. A few people have worked it into their own routines, but there's no team standard (2 pts)
- C. AI is part of some defined workflows, but coverage is inconsistent across functions (3 pts)
- D. AI is embedded in core workflows with clear inputs, outputs, and review steps (4 pts)

**Question 3: Quality & Governance**
"Who owns the quality of AI-generated work, and how is it governed?"

- A. No one, there's no standard or review process (1 pt)
- B. Individuals review their own outputs informally (2 pts)
- C. Some workflows have quality expectations, but no consistent owner or standard (3 pts)
- D. Output quality is owned, held to documented standards, and reviewed before it goes out the door (4 pts)

**Question 4: Measurement**
"How do you measure what AI is actually worth to the business?"

- A. We don't measure it at all (1 pt)
- B. We have anecdotes, but no numbers (2 pts)
- C. We track some usage metrics, but they aren't tied to time saved or dollar value (3 pts)
- D. We measure time saved and business impact, and leadership sees it on a regular cadence (4 pts)

**Question 5: Institutional Memory**
"Does your organization's AI get smarter over time, or does every session start from scratch?"

- A. Every session starts from scratch, we haven't built anything persistent (1 pt)
- B. A few people have saved personal prompts, but nothing is shared (2 pts)
- C. We have some shared templates, but they're not consistently maintained (3 pts)
- D. We've built a living knowledge base that compounds over time: prompts, role libraries, context (4 pts)

**Question 6: Adoption & Scaling**
"What's your model for spreading AI use beyond the people who figured it out on their own?"

- A. None, adoption is whoever happens to be curious (1 pt)
- B. There's informal sharing, but no plan to scale it across the team (2 pts)
- C. Some training or enablement exists, but it's uneven across functions (3 pts)
- D. A clear model: enablement, role-based tracks, and leadership visibility into adoption (4 pts)

### Context Questions

These are not scored. Use the answers to tailor the output.

**Question 7: Productivity Suite**
"Which productivity suite does your team primarily use?"

- Microsoft 365
- Google Workspace
- A mix of both
- Neither / Other

**Question 8: IT Infrastructure**
"How do you manage your IT infrastructure and support?"

- In-house IT team or IT Manager
- Third-party managed service provider (MSP)
- A combination of both
- No formal IT support in place

## Scoring

Calculate the total from questions 1-6 (range: 6-24).

| Score | Stage | What It Means |
|-------|-------|---------------|
| 6-11 | Foundation | Ad hoc at best. The basics need to be in place before any initiative will stick. |
| 12-17 | Building | Some foundations exist, but gaps are wide enough to break a new initiative. Prioritize the lowest-scoring dimensions before committing budget. |
| 18-21 | Advancing | Solid in places. Ready to move, but specific gaps will create friction. Address them in parallel with your build. |
| 22-24 | Compounding | Operating at a level where new AI initiatives can compound on what's already working. Focus on scale and governance. |

Present the output in this structure:

**AI Readiness Score: [X] / 24 ([Stage Name])**

**What's Working**
Name the 1-2 dimensions where the score was highest. Be specific about what they have.

**Where the Gaps Are**
Name the 1-2 lowest-scoring dimensions. Explain why each gap matters in plain language and what it will cost them if they start an initiative without closing it. If Security scored a 1 or 2, lead with it: an unsecured AI rollout is the fastest way to turn an advantage into a liability.

**What to Do Before You Move**
One to three specific actions, sized for what they actually need. Not a reading list. Actionable, specific, doable in the next 30 days.

## Tone Notes

Keep the diagnostic conversational, not clinical. Ask one question at a time. If the user's answer doesn't map cleanly to an option, use your judgment to score it based on the closest match and note any nuance in the output. The goal is a useful snapshot, not a perfect score. Be direct about the gaps. Don't soften the output to be polite -- an honest read is what makes the diagnostic worth running.
